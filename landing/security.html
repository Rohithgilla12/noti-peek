Security, responsibly.

Last updated · 2026-04-22

Found a vulnerability? Report it privately to security@notipeek.dev before disclosing publicly. We'll acknowledge within 72 hours and work with you on a fix timeline. Acting in good faith under this policy, we won't pursue legal action.

§ 1How to report

Email security@notipeek.dev

Please include: a description of the issue, steps to reproduce (or a proof-of-concept), the affected version or endpoint, and whether you've already disclosed to anyone else. PGP is not required — if you need end-to-end encryption, open a minimal GitHub issue asking for a key and we'll respond with one off-list.

§ 2Scope

In scope:

  • The hosted backend at api.notipeek.dev (Cloudflare Worker + D1).
  • The desktop app distributed from noti-peek-releases, including Tauri configuration, OAuth flows, and token storage.
  • The source code in the main repo.
  • This landing page and its subdomains.

Out of scope:

  • Third-party providers (GitHub, Linear, Jira, Bitbucket) — report to them directly.
  • Cloudflare infrastructure — report via Cloudflare's abuse form.
  • Self-hosted deployments of the backend — those are on the operator.
  • Missing best-practice headers, CSP bypasses without user impact, clickjacking without sensitive action, or other low-severity theoretical issues.

§ 3Safe harbor

We authorize good-faith security research on the in-scope systems above. We will not pursue legal action for research that:

  • Does not degrade service for other users (no DoS, no fuzzing production at scale).
  • Does not access, modify, or destroy data belonging to another user.
  • Uses only your own accounts and device tokens for testing.
  • Follows this disclosure policy and gives us reasonable time to fix before publishing.

If in doubt about whether a test crosses the line, email first — we'd rather answer a clarifying question than argue afterwards.

§ 4Response timeline

This is an indie project maintained part-time, but we take security seriously:

  • Within 72 hours: acknowledgement of receipt.
  • Within 7 days: initial assessment — confirmed / not-reproducible / need-more-info.
  • Within 30 days: fix deployed for confirmed high/critical issues. Lower-severity issues may take longer.

Fix windows can stretch if a vulnerability requires upstream coordination (e.g. a Tauri or Hono bug). We will keep you in the loop and coordinate public disclosure.

§ 5After a fix

Confirmed issues are tracked in a private advisory on GitHub until patched, then promoted to a public security advisory. With your permission, we credit reporters by name (or handle) in the advisory and release notes. If you prefer to stay anonymous, that's fine too.

§ 6What we don't offer

No bug bounty program. noti-peek is free, open-source, and indie-funded — we can't pay cash rewards. We can offer credit, swag-equivalents once those exist, and a genuine thank-you.

Please do not open public GitHub issues, post to social media, or email providers about a vulnerability before reporting to us and giving us a chance to fix it. Coordinated disclosure protects users.

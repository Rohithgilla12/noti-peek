# noti-peek — Design Context

## Users

**Who:** developers with active work on at least two of GitHub, Linear, Jira, Bitbucket. Heavy keyboard users. Multi-monitor. Usually wearing headphones. Already have a menubar row occupied by 12 other icons.

**Context of use:** 400×550 transparent window attached to a tray icon. Invoked by mouse or shortcut. Typical session is **3–15 seconds**: click the tray, glance, either dismiss (`Esc`) or act on one thing and close. It is NOT a dashboard. It is NOT a place the user lives.

**Job to be done:** "without context-switching out of my current flow, tell me what new things happened across my dev tools that I actually need to act on — and let me dismiss the rest."

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** *terminal · calm · unshowy*

**Voice:** the landing page copy — "Built to disappear into your workflow" — is the brand. Technical, declarative, self-assured. No excitement, no exclamation marks, no "supercharge your" language.

**Emotional goal:** when a user opens noti-peek and there's nothing to do, the app should feel like an **empty desk at the end of the day**, not an "all caught up 🎉" party. When there's a stack of notifications, it should feel like a **well-organized index card box**, not a feed.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Signature look (chosen direction):** one signature noti-peek appearance, replacing the 13-theme buffet. One primary look + one light variant for people who work in bright rooms. "Terminal, calm" — the deep aesthetic lineage is man-page typography and monochrome monospace CRT software, not neon-y terminal emulators.

**Anti-references:** Raycast (too polished, too much chrome), Superhuman (too glossy, too product-marketing), Slack/Discord-style feeds (too social, too loud), Catppuccin purple glow and Synthwave '84 neon (common AI dev-tool aesthetic), "glassmorphism + gradient" dev tool skins.

**Yes-references (directionally):** ABC Diatype / Söhne Mono label sets, OpenBSD man pages in 2004, the restraint of NotionCal's information density, a terminal multiplexer status bar — but with color restraint and real typographic care, not 90s monospace-everything.

**Theme:** primarily dark. Menubar invoked at 11pm with JetBrains in the other window. Light variant exists for daylight but is not the primary canvas.

## Design Principles

1. **Reward the glance.** The first 400ms of attention is all we get. What's new and what's mine should be legible before the user consciously reads.
2. **Density is a feature.** This audience reads `git log --oneline` willingly. Tight leading, aligned columns, meaningful abbreviation. Do not inflate things with empty space to look "premium."
3. **Chrome earns its pixels.** Every divider, chip, and avatar must be load-bearing. If it's decorative in a 550px-tall window, it's deleted.
4. **Motion is feedback, not performance.** Animations fire when state changes (mark read, new arrival, bundle expand). Nothing animates just to be seen.
5. **Nothing neon.** The signature look avoids the cyan-on-dark / neon-green-terminal cliché. The palette is warm-gray on desaturated ink, with exactly one accent hue earning the 10% of 60/30/10.
